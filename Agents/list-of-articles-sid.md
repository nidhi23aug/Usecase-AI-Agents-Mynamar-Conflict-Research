**Remote Sensing & Machine Learning for Cropland Abandonment**

**1. Global & Regional Reviews**

A global review of monitoring cropland abandonment using remote sensing: Temporal–spatial patterns, causes, ecological effects, and future prospects
Authors: T. Liu, L. Yu, X. Liu, et al. (Journal of Remote Sensing, 2025)
Focus: Systematically classifies monitoring methods into image spectral features and land cover temporal trajectories.

The progress and potential directions in the remote sensing of farmland abandonment
Authors: A.V. Prishchepov, K. Anders, J. Feranec, et al. (Remote Sensing of Environment, 2025)
Focus: Synthesizes 131 publications to assess progress and identifies data source limitations (optical vs. multi-source).

A review of the application of remote sensing data for abandoned agricultural land identification (Central/Eastern Europe)
Authors: T. Goga, J. Feranec, T. Bucha, et al. (Remote Sensing, 2019)
Focus: Analyzes 73 studies; emphasizes the fusion of Sentinel-1 (Radar) and Sentinel-2 (Optical) data.

Status, trend, and prospect of global farmland abandonment research: a bibliometric analysis
Authors: B. Liu, W. Song, Q. Sun (Int. Journal of Env. Research and Public Health, 2022)
Focus: Highlights the shift toward high spatial-temporal resolution and hyperspectral data integration.

Review of methods and approaches of abandoned lands identification
Authors: E. Barbotkina, I.E. Dunaieva, et al. (E3S Web of Conferences, 2020)
Focus: Explores MODIS time-series analysis combined with ground data for identification.

**2. Deep Learning & Transformer Architectures**

Improved cropland abandonment detection with deep learning vision transformer (DL-ViT) and multiple vegetation indices
Authors: M. Karim, J. Deng, M. Ayoub, et al. (Land, 2023)
Focus: Integrates Vision Transformers (ViT) with Gaofen-6 imagery for precise discrimination.

Deep Learning and Remote Sensing for Restoring Abandoned Agricultural Lands in the Middle Volga (Russia)
Authors: A. Gafurov, M. Ivanov (Land, 2024)
Focus: Employs MAnet architecture with a Mix Vision Transformer encoder (MiT-b5) for semantic segmentation.

Detecting land abandonment in Łódź Voivodeship using convolutional neural networks
Authors: S. Krysiak, E. Papińska, et al. (Land, 2020)
Focus: Proposes a CNN architecture for automatic detection using Sentinel Hub multispectral data.

Abandoned land mapping based on spatiotemporal features from PolSAR data via deep learning methods
Authors: Y. Yang, Z. Wu, W. Xiao, et al. (Remote Sensing, 2023)
Focus: Hybrid framework using VGG16 (spatial) and LSTM (time-series) for Radar-based classification.

Statistical texture learning method for monitoring abandoned suburban cropland based on high-resolution remote sensing and deep learning
Authors: Q. Shen, H. Deng, X. Wen, et al. (IEEE J-STARS, 2023)
Focus: Introduces PSPNet-STL, combining high-level semantic features with low-level texture mining.

Putting Abandoned Farmlands in the Legend of Land Use and Land Cover Maps of the Brazilian Tropical Savanna
Authors: I.A.L. Magalhães, E.E. Sano, et al. (Land, 2025)
Focus: First deep learning-based assessment of the Brazilian Cerrado using Fully Connected Neural Networks (FCNN).

Soil Management and Machine Learning Abandonment Detection in Mediterranean Olive Groves Under Drought
Authors: G. Marchese, J.E. Herranz-Luque, et al. (Soil Systems, 2025)
Focus: Applies the YOLOv12 object detection model to high-resolution orthophotos.

**3. Interpretable ML & Optimization Frameworks**

Identification of Abandoned Cropland and Global–Local Driving Mechanism Analysis via Multi-Source Remote Sensing Data
Authors: S. Gui, J. Li, G. Chen, et al. (Remote Sensing, 2025)
Focus: Combines XGBoost with NSGA-II optimization; uses SHAP/LIME for model interpretability.

Monitoring of Farmland Abandonment Based on Google Earth Engine and Interpretable Machine Learning
Authors: Y. Jiang, Y. Jiang, X. Guo, et al. (Agriculture, 2025)
Focus: Utilizes an XGBoost–SHAP framework to quantify contributions of soil, precipitation, and terrain features.

Detecting Abandoned Cropland in Monsoon-Influenced Regions Using HLS Imagery and Interpretable Machine Learning
Authors: S. Park, S. Kang, B. Hwang, D.W. Ko (Agronomy, 2025)
Focus: Leverages Harmonized Landsat Sentinel (HLS) data to manage cloud-cover issues in monsoon climates.

An OVR-FWP-RF machine learning algorithm for identification of abandoned farmland in hilly areas
Authors: L. Wang, Q. Li, et al. (Sustainability, 2024)
Focus: Improves Random Forest accuracy in complex terrains using multispectral feature factors.

**4. Time-Series Analysis & Change Detection**

Cropland abandonment between 1986 and 2018 across the United States: spatiotemporal patterns and current land uses
Authors: Y. Xie, S.A. Spawn-Lee, V.C. Radeloff, et al. (Environmental Research Letters, 2024)
Focus: Nationwide 30m resolution map of the US using Landsat and automated classification in GEE.

A Full-Life-Cycle Modeling Framework for Cropland Abandonment Detection Based on Dense Time Series
Authors: Q. Sun, Z. You, P. Zhang, et al. (Remote Sensing, 2025)
Focus: Uses spectral mixture models to generate annual fractional vegetation/soil endmembers over 23 years.

Mapping abandoned farmland in China using time series MODIS NDVI
Authors: X. Zhu, G. Xiao, D. Zhang, et al. (Science of the Total Environment, 2021)
Focus: Applies C5.0 decision tree classification on MODIS trajectories (2002–2017).

Remote sensing monitoring of cropland abandonment at the parcel level based on time-series fitting
Authors: X. Yang, Y. Liu, H. Li, et al. (Ecological Informatics, 2026)
Focus: Integrates OBIA–LT and LandTrendr to identify the timing and dynamics of abandonment at the field level.

Detecting abandoned farmland using harmonic analysis and machine learning
Authors: H. Yoon, S. Kim (ISPRS Journal of Photogrammetry and Remote Sensing, 2020)
Focus: Uses SVM and harmonic analysis of vegetation indices (NDVI, SAVI, NDWI) for classification.

Revealing the distribution and change of abandoned cropland in Ukraine based on dual period change detection method
Authors: S. Zhang, Y. Zhang, et al. (Scientific Reports, 2025)
Focus: Proposes Dual-period Change Detection (DCD) to identify land status in conflict-affected areas.

**5. Case Studies: China & Conflict Zones**
Mapping the spatiotemporal dynamics of cropland abandonment and recultivation across the Yangtze River Basin
Authors: Y. Long, J. Sun, et al. (Remote Sensing, 2024)
Focus: Maps both abandonment and recultivation trends (2000–2020) via GEE.

Monitoring of cropland abandonment based on long time series: A case study of Fujian Province, China
Authors: J. Wu, S. Jin, G. Zhu, J. Guo (Agronomy, 2023)
Focus: Applies Random Forest and LandTrendr to extract abandonment drivers like urbanization and road access.

Using machine learning and remote sensing to track land use/land cover changes due to armed conflict (Syria)
Authors: S. Mhanna, L.J.S. Halloran, et al. (Science of the Total Environment, 2023)
Focus: Quantifies cropland decreases in conflict hotspots, linking reduction to groundwater and migration.

Agricultural land abandonment and retirement mapping in the northern China crop-pasture band
Authors: Z. Du, J. Yang, C. Ou, T. Zhang (IEEE TGRS, 2021)
Focus: Distinguishes between land abandonment and retirement using trajectory-based change detection.

Understanding the spatiotemporal patterns of seasonal, annual, and consecutive farmland abandonment in China
Authors: L. Li, Y. Pan, R. Zheng, et al. (Land Degradation & Development, 2022)
Focus: Analyzes types of abandonment, noting seasonal abandonment accounts for >50% of the total in China.
